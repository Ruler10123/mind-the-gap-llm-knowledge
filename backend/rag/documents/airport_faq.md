# Airport Frequently Asked Questions

## Baggage Policies

### Carry-On Baggage
Passengers are allowed one carry-on bag and one personal item. Carry-on bags must not exceed 22 x 14 x 9 inches (56 x 36 x 23 cm) and must fit in the overhead bin. Personal items include purses, laptop bags, and small backpacks.

### Checked Baggage
Standard checked baggage allowance is one bag up to 50 lbs (23 kg) for economy class and two bags for business and first class. Additional bags incur fees. Oversized or overweight bags require special handling and additional charges.

### Prohibited Items
Prohibited items in carry-on baggage include liquids over 3.4 oz (100ml), sharp objects, firearms, and explosive materials. All liquids must be in containers of 3.4 oz or less and placed in a single quart-sized clear plastic bag.

### Lost Baggage
If your baggage is lost or delayed, report it immediately at the baggage service office located in the baggage claim area. You will need your baggage claim ticket and flight information. The airline will track your bag and deliver it to you when located.

## Security Procedures

### TSA Screening
All passengers must go through TSA security screening. Have your boarding pass and ID ready. Remove shoes, belts, jackets, and electronic devices larger than a cell phone from your bag. Place all items in bins for X-ray screening.

### Liquids Rule
The 3-1-1 rule applies: 3.4 ounces (100ml) or less per container, all containers must fit in 1 quart-sized clear plastic bag, 1 bag per passenger.

### PreCheck
TSA PreCheck members can use expedited security lanes and do not need to remove shoes, belts, light jackets, laptops, or compliant liquids from bags. PreCheck is available for U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents.

### International Travel
For international flights, arrive at least 3 hours before departure. You may need to go through additional security screening and customs. Have your passport and any required visas ready.

## Airport Services

### WiFi
Free WiFi is available throughout the airport. Connect to the "Airport_Free_WiFi" network. Premium high-speed WiFi is available for purchase.

### Dining and Shopping
The airport features over 50 restaurants, cafes, and bars, plus 30+ retail shops. Dining options range from fast food to sit-down restaurants. Shops include duty-free, travel essentials, electronics, and souvenirs.

### Lounges
Airport lounges are available for first class, business class, and lounge membership holders. Lounges offer complimentary food, beverages, WiFi, workstations, and quiet relaxation areas. Day passes can be purchased at the lounge entrance.

### Currency Exchange
Currency exchange services are located in the international terminal near gates C and D. ATMs are available throughout the airport and accept major credit and debit cards.

### Charging Stations
Free charging stations with USB and standard outlets are located throughout the terminal near seating areas and gates. Look for the blue "Charging Station" signs.

## Facilities

### Restrooms
Restrooms are located throughout the terminal, typically near gate areas and food courts. Family restrooms with changing tables are available. All facilities are ADA compliant.

### Help Desk
Information desks staffed by airport ambassadors are located in the main terminal near security checkpoints and in each concourse. Staff can help with directions, flight information, and general questions.

### Medical Services
A first aid station is located in the main terminal near Gate B15. For medical emergencies, call 911 or use the emergency phones located throughout the terminal.

### Lost and Found
The Lost and Found office is located in the main terminal near baggage claim. Items found in the airport are held for 90 days. Contact the office at (555) 123-4567 or visit in person.

### Pet Relief Areas
Pet relief areas are located pre-security near the main entrance and post-security near Gates A10, C20, and D30. These areas feature synthetic grass and waste disposal facilities.

## Flight Information

### Check-In
Online check-in opens 24 hours before departure. Airport kiosks and airline counters open 3 hours before domestic flights and 4 hours before international flights. Boarding typically begins 30-45 minutes before departure.

### Gate Information
Gate assignments are displayed on departure boards throughout the terminal and on your boarding pass. Gates may change, so check monitors regularly and listen for announcements.

### Boarding Process
Boarding is done by groups called zones. Your zone is printed on your boarding pass. First class, business class, and elite status members typically board first, followed by general boarding zones.

### Flight Delays and Cancellations
In case of delays or cancellations, check with your airline's customer service desk or mobile app for rebooking options. Airlines are required to provide accommodations for certain types of delays.

## Special Assistance

### Wheelchair Service
Wheelchair assistance is available free of charge. Request wheelchair service when booking your flight or at least 48 hours in advance. Wheelchairs are also available at the ticket counter on a first-come basis.

### Traveling with Children
Family lanes are available at security checkpoints. Strollers can be gate-checked at no charge. Children under 2 years old can travel on a parent's lap or in their own seat with an approved car seat.

### Service Animals
Service animals are permitted throughout the airport and on flights. Emotional support animals require advance notification to the airline. Pet relief areas are available both before and after security.

### Language Assistance
Airport staff members fluent in Spanish, Mandarin, and French are available at information desks. Translation services via phone are available for other languages.

## Parking and Ground Transportation

### Parking
Short-term parking is available in the garage near the terminal ($4/hour, $32/day). Long-term parking is in the economy lot ($12/day) with free shuttle service to the terminal. Reserved parking can be booked online.

### Rideshare
Uber and Lyft pickup is on Level 1 (Arrivals) in the designated rideshare zone. Follow signs for "Rideshare Pickup" and match your vehicle before boarding.

### Taxis
Taxi stands are located outside baggage claim on Level 1. Taxis operate on metered fares. Average fare to downtown is $45-60.

### Public Transit
The airport rail station connects to the city metro system. Trains run every 15 minutes from 5 AM to midnight. The station is accessible via the pedestrian bridge from Terminal 1.

### Rental Cars
Rental car counters are located in the main terminal near baggage claim. All major rental car companies are represented. Rental car return is at the garage on Level 2.
